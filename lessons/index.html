Private Lessons

TCHA has partnered with a number of private music instructors as resources for our community to develop skills in various instruments.

Instructors

  • Emily Pantel

    Violin, Viola, Cello, Bass
    epantel@gmail.com

    Emily Pantel graduated from the University of Northwestern, St. Paul in 2007 with a degree in music education. She enjoys playing the violin and sharing her love of music with her students. While the violin is her primary instrument, Emily also enjoys teaching beginning levels of viola, cello, and bass. As an educator, she is committed to meeting students where they are and addressing their individual learning needs.

    A native of the Twin Cities, Emily has spent significant time living in Switzerland and Germany with her family. She is always eager to embrace new experiences and has held various roles, including mother, orchestra personnel manager, private string teacher, orchestra conductor, church service coordinator, and Sunday school teacher. In January 2024, Emily and her family relocated from Switzerland to Eagan.

    Among her favorite activities are baking bread, spending time with her family, and playing the violin with the Metropolitan Symphony Orchestra. Emily welcomes those interested in starting their musical journey to contact her for a free trial lesson. She looks forward to meeting new students and helping them discover the joys of music.

  • Eva Opseth

    Voice
    evaopsethstudio@gmail.com

    Eva Opseth is a Voice Professional who has experience in teaching and performing throughout the Twin Cities and upper Midwest. Eva has a Bachelor’s degree in Music (with an emphasis in Vocal Performance) and over 10 years of vocal training. Her professional experience includes performing at Shell Lake Arts Center, University of Wisconsin River Falls, Orchestra Hall and more. She is a TCHA alumni and was involved in choir and theater during her time as a student. Her experience on stage has shaped her teaching pedagogy and given her skills to help her students pursue their passions and grow their abilities. Eva currently teaches private voice and piano lessons from her home studio as well as Concert Choir at Journey Homeschool Group in River Falls, WI. In lessons, Eva helps students create high and achievable goals. She is passionate about equipping students with not only musical knowledge and vocal techniques, but also rehearsal tips, vocal anatomy, and portraying a character through song.

  • Jaylin Hustedde

    Acting and Audition Coaching
    jaylin.hustedde@tcha-mn.com

    Do you have an audition coming up? Or are you in a show and want to bounce ideas off of an educated theatre artist for a specific scene or song? Maybe you are looking at college programs and want some advice on your technical portfolio, or you are looking for fresh new material to add to your book of monologues and songs!

    If this resonates with you in any way, consider booking a private coaching session with theatre artist and teacher Jaylin Hustedde. Jaylin is a performer, director, choreographer, and theatre teacher here in the Twin Cities with a passion for helping develop the next generation of theatre artists. One of her theatre mottos is that as an actor you are too inside the frame to see the picture. Meaning everyone needs an outside pair of eyes to help guide your craft and help you develop performances you can be proud of.

    For $45 you can book 1 hour of private coaching with Jaylin to work on any of the following areas,

    • Audition Material Preparation
    • Resume and Portfolio Review
    • Finding Audition Postings and Specific Material
    • Finding New Content - Monologues and Music for Auditions

    Voice
    lisabernard9@gmail.com

    Lisa Bernard has sung professionally for over 25 years. Her voice has appeared on numerous radio/TV commercials and she’s recorded background vocals for many major Pop and Christian recording artists.   • Lizzie Zanto

    Dance
    lizziezanto@gmail.com

    Private Dance Lessons (8 - 18 years old)

    Class Description

    Private lessons are a great opportunity for any student looking for dedicated 1-on-1 teaching. During these lessons, students can work towards specific skills and goals with the assistance of a focused instructor not having to balance the needs of multiple students. Private lessons are available for the following styles and levels:

    Beginner Tap - Tap shoes requiredAdvanced Beginner Tap - Tap shoes requiredMusical Theatre Dance - Jazz shoes requiredJazz I & II - Jazz shoes required

    Private lessons are 1-on-1, maximum of 3 for small group lessons. No dance experience required for private lessons - Just a willingness to learn, work hard and have fun!

    Course Goals

    Some potential goals students may have that private lessons support:

    • Upcoming audition or performance prep, specific skill mastery, faster progression, assistance learning a specific routine, improve choreography retention speed, performance in dance (Face/body expression and style), and student-tailored lessons.

    To schedule a private lesson or learn more information, please contact the instructor at lizziezanto@gmail.com. Please include "Private Dance Lesson" and the name(s) of the student in the subject line.

  • Michelle Rose

    Voice, Piano, Composition (In-Home)
    michelle.rose@tcha-mn.com
    651-900-1729

    Michelle has been a seeker of creativity & wonderment since childhood. Raised in Madagascar and Minnesota, as a young girl Michelle fell in love with the internal landscape of books and story, and the external narrative of nature and the outdoors, with music woven throughout both worlds. As a teenager she filled her days with theater, choir, speech, and music and found in those spaces both her strengths and gifting, as well as her people and belonging.

    Michelle received a BA in Music Performance with a vocal emphasis from Colorado Christian University, and spent the following years touring with various groups and working as a studio musician and vocalist for commercial and corporate events. Michelle has also led worship for over 25 years, recorded on various worship albums featuring her original music, and released two solo albums as a singer-songwriter.

    Teaching voice and piano from her home studio, Michelle has also taught and mentored songwriting for several summers at The Junior Composer’s Institute, and directed four years of Christmas choir and orchestra performances of Handel’s Messiah.

    In 2013 Michelle joined the founding board of Twin Cities Homeschoolers for the Arts and is passionate about providing faith-based quality performing arts opportunities to the homeschooling community. Michelle has a special love for working with teens, enjoying the role of music director for TCHA musical productions since 2018, and is now thrilled to be directing the TCHA teen mixed choirs and female select ensemble. She has two college-age children, Kenoy and Paris, and lives in Eagan, MN.

  • Spencer Shoeneman

    Drums (In-Home)
    https://www.inthekeyofrhythm.com/lessons

    Being a home school alum myself, I am excited to re-engage with the home school community; this time as an educator. I graduated in 2014 from McNally Smith College of Music with a Bachelor’s of Music in Percussion Performance.
